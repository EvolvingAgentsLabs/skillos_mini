# Cepstral Analysis\n\n## Definition\nCepstral analysis is the process of taking the inverse Fourier transform of the logarithm of the spectrum of a signal. The resulting "cepstrum" is used to separate the source excitation from the filter response (impulse response).\n\n## Mathematical Derivation\nFor a signal $x[n]$, the Fourier Transform is $X(f) = \\mathcal{F}\{{x[n]}\\}$. In arterial pressure analysis, $x[n]$ represents the convolution of the arterial system response $h[n]$ and the cardiac pulse $s[n]$:\n\n$$x[n] = s[n] * h[n] \\implies X(f) = S(f) \\cdot H(f)$$\n\nTo separate $s[n]$ and $h[n]$, we apply the complex cepstrum operation:\n\n1. **Log-Magnitude Spectrum:** $L(f) = \\ln |X(f)|\<br>\n2. **Inverse Fourier Transform:** $c[n] = \\mathcal{F}^{-1}\\{L(f)\<$$\n\nBy applying the logarithm, the multiplicative relationship in the frequency domain becomes additive:\n\n$$\\ln |X(f)| = \\ln |S(f)| + \\ln |H(f)|$$\n\nIn the cepstral domain, convolution becomes addition, allowing for [[concepts/homomorphic-signal-separation]] via liftering (filtering in the cepstral domain).\n\n## Key Properties\n- **Homomorphic Property:** Converts multiplicative structures into additive ones.\n- **Deconvolution:** Enables separation of the arterial reflection (echoes) from the primary pulse.\n- **Liftering:** Frequency-domain filtering applied to the cepstrum.\n\n## How It Works\nIn the quantum regime, we aim to implement this using [[concepts/quantum-fourier-transform]] and quantum arithmetic for the $\\ln$ function, potentially via [[concepts/quantum-singular-value-transformation]].\n\n## Open Questions\n- Efficient implementation of the $\\ln$ function on a quantum computer without collapsing the superposition.\n- Stability of the quantum cepstrum under stochastic noise modeling of arterial walls.
