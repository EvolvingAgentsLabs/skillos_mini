# Mathematical Framework: Quantum Homomorphic Cepstral Analysis\n\n## 1. Signal Model\nWe model the observed arterial pressure signal $s(t)$ as a combination of the primary cardiac pulse $p(t)$ and a reflected echo.\n\n**GIVEN:**\n- $p(t)$: The primary, low-frequency cardiac pressure pulse.\n- $\\alpha$: The attenuation coefficient ($0 < \\alpha < 1$) representing energy lost during reflection.\n- $\\tau$: The time delay representing the travel time to the bifurcation and back.\n\n**DERIVE:** The composite signal $s(t)$\n$$\\begin{aligned}\ns(t) &= p(t) + \\alpha \\cdot p(t - \\tau)\n\\end{aligned}$$\n\n## 2. Frequency Domain Representation\nTo isolate the echo, we transform the signal into the frequency domain using the Fourier Transform $\\mathcal{F}$.\n\n**GIVEN:**\n- $S(\\omega) = \\mathcal{F}\\{s(t)\\}$\n- $P(\\omega) = \\mathcal{F}\\{p(t)\|\n\n**DERIVE:** The spectral representation $S(\\omega)$\n$$\\begin{aligned}\nS(\\omega) &= P(\\omega) + \\alpha \\cdot P(\\omega)e^{-i\\omega\\tau}\\\\ \nS(\\omega) &= P(\\omega)(1 + \\alpha e^{-i\\omega\\tau})\\\\\n|S(\\omega)| &= |P(\\omega)| \\cdot |1 + \\alpha e^{-i\\omega\\tau}|\\\\\n\\end{aligned}$$\n\nThe term $|1 + \\alpha e^{-i\\omega\\tau}|$ introduces a periodic modulation (ripple) across the spectrum of $P(\\omega)$.\n\n## 3. Homomorphic Decomposition (Cepstral Domain)\nTo separate the pulse shape from the echo modulation, we apply a homomorphic transformation.\n\n**STEP 1: Logarithmic Compression**\nWe take the logarithm of the magnitude spectrum to convert the multiplicative relationship into an additive one:\n$$\\begin{aligned}\n\\log|S(\\omega)| &= \\log|P(\\omega)| + \\log|1 + \\alpha e^{-i\\omega\\tau}|\\\\\n\\end{aligned}$$\n\n**STEP 2: Inverse Fourier Transform (The Cepstrum)**\nWe apply the Inverse Fourier Transform to the log-spectrum to move into the 'quefrency' domain $c(t_q)$:\n$$\\begin{aligned}\nc(t_q) &= \\mathcal{F}^{-1}\\{\\log|S(\\omega)|\\}\\\\ \nc(t_q) &= \\mathcal{F}^{-1}\\{\\log|P(\\omega)|\\} + \\mathcal{F}^{-1}\\{\\log|1 + \\alpha e^{-i\\omega\\tau}|\\}\\\\ \n\\end{aligned}$$\n\n**QED:** The second term $\\mathcal{F}^{-1}\\{\\log|1 + \\alpha e^{-i\\omega\\tau}|\\}$ results in a sharp impulse (peak) at $t_q = \\tau$. Identifying this peak yields the distance to the arterial bifurcation.\n\n## 4. System Dynamics Notation\nIn modeling the hemodynamics of the arterial segment, we define the following stocks and flows:\n\n- **[STOCK]** $V_{vessel}$: Volume of blood within the arterial segment.\n- **[FLOW]** $Q_{in}$: Inflow rate from the heart (cardiac output).\n- **[FLOW]** $Q_{out}$: Outflow rate to distal vessels.\n- **[FB+]** Positive feedback: Increased pressure leads to increased vessel wall tension, slightly increasing resistance.\n- **[FB-]** Negative feedback: Increased wall tension leads to vessel expansion, reducing resistance and stabilizing flow.\n\n$$\\begin{aligned}\n\\frac{dV_{vessel}}{dt} &= Q_{in} - Q_{out}\n\\end{aligned}$$\n\n## 5. Quantum Translation Requirements\nThe implementation must map these steps to quantum primitives:\n1. **State Preparation**: $|s\\rangle = \\sum s_n |n\\rangle$\n2. **Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT)**: $|s\\rangle \\xrightarrow{QFT} |S\\rangle$\n3. **Quantum Logarithm Approximation**: Implementing a unitary that approximates the $\\log$ of amplitudes.\n4. **Inverse QFT**: To recover the cepstral peaks in the quantum register.
