# Project Echo-Q: Mathematical Foundations Wiki\n\n## Overview\nThis wiki serves as the rigorous mathematical foundation for the development of a quantum cepstral deconvolution algorithm designed for echo separation in arterial pressure waves. The objective is to utilize quantum algorithms to disentangle reflected waves (echoes) from primary physiological pressure signals.\n\n---\n\n## [[concepts/quantum-fourier-transform]]\n\n### Definition\nThe Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT) is the quantum analogue of the Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT). For a quantum state $|x\\rangle$ where $x \in \\mathbb{Z}_{2^n}$, the QFT maps it to:\n\n$$|x\\rangle \\rightarrow \\frac{1}{\\sqrt{2^n}} \\sum_{k=0}^{2^n-1} e^{2\\pi i x k / 2^n} |k\\rangle$$\n\n### Key Properties\n1. **Exponential Speedup**: While the classical FFT requires $O(N \\log N)$ operations, the QFT requires $O(\\log^2 N)$ gates.\n2. **Unitary Evolution**: The QFT is a unitary transformation, preserving the norm of the quantum state.\n3. **Phase Encoding**: It maps amplitude information into phase information, which is critical for [[concepts/cepstral-analysis]].\n\n### How It Works\nThe QFT is implemented via a sequence of Hadamard gates and controlled-phase rotation gates $R_k$, where:\n\n$$R_k = \\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\\\ 0 & e^{2\\pi i / 2^k} \\end{pmatrix}$$\n\nThe circuit applies these rotations to encode the frequency components into the relative phases of the qubits.\n\n### Open Questions\n- How to mitigate decoherence during the long rotation sequences required for high-resolution arterial signal decomposition?\n- Optimal measurement strategies to extract the classical Fourier coefficients without collapsing the entire superposition.\n\n---\n\n## [[concepts/quantum-singular-value-transformation]]\n\n### Definition\nQuantum Singular Value Transformation (QSVT) is a unified framework for applying polynomial transformations to the singular values of a block-encoded matrix. Given a matrix $A$ block-encoded in a larger unitary $U$, QSVT applies a polynomial $P(x)$ such that $U$ becomes a transformation mapping $A \\rightarrow P(A)$.\n\n### Key Properties\n1. **Universality**: QSVT can perform Hamiltonian simulation, matrix inversion, and signal processing via different polynomial choices.\n2. **Precision Scaling**: Error scaling is logarithmic with respect to the required precision $\\epsilon$.\n\n### How It Works\nBy using a sequence of signal rotations and phase factors $\\phi$, one can construct a polynomial $P(x)$ that approximates the desired function. In the context of Echo-Q, this allows for the direct application of deconvolution filters in the quantum domain.\n\n### Open Questions\n- Determining the optimal degree of the polynomial $P(x)$ to minimize gate depth while maintaining sufficient spectral resolution for arterial pulse waves.\n\n---\n\n## [[concepts/block-encoding]]\n\n### Definition\nA matrix $A$ is said to be $\\epsilon$-block-encoded in a unitary $U$ if:\n\n$$U = \\begin\\begin{pmatrix} A/\\alpha & \\dots \\\\ \\dots & \\dots \\end{pmatrix}$$\n\nwhere $\\alpha$ is a scaling factor. This allows us to embed classical linear operators into quantum hardware.\n\n### Key Properties\n1. **Normalization**: Requires $\\alpha \\ge \\|A\\|_2$.\n2. **Foundation for QSVT**: Block encoding is the prerequisite for applying any polynomial transformation to a matrix.\n\n### How It Works\nUsing techniques like LCU (Linear Combination of Unitaries), we can construct $U$ such that the top-left block corresponds to our signal matrix $A$ (e.g., the convolution kernel of the arterial wall).\n\n---\n\n## [[concepts/cepstral-analysis]]\n\n### Definition\nCepstral analysis involves taking the inverse Fourier transform of the logarithm of the spectrum of a signal. In the context of arterial pressure waves, the signal $s(t)$ is modeled as:\n\n$$s(t) = p(t) * h(t)$$\n\nwhere $p(t)$ is the primary pressure wave and $h(t)$ is the impulse response of the arterial system (including echoes).\n\n### Mathematical Derivation\n1. **Fourier Transform**: $S(f) = P(f) \\cdot H(f)$\n2. **Log-Spectrum**: $\\log(S(f)) = \\log(P(f)) + \\log(H(f))$\n3. **Inverse Fourier Transform (Cepstrum)**: $c(\\tau) = \\mathcal{F}^{-1}\{-\\log(S(f))\\}$\n\nThe deconvolution is achieved because the convolution in the time domain becomes addition in the cepstral domain.\n\n### Relation to Quantum Algorithms\nUsing QFT, we can compute the log-spectrum of a quantum-encoded signal. The challenge lies in the $\\log$ operation, which requires [[concepts/quantum-singular-value-transformation]].\n\n---\n\n## [[concepts/homomorphic-signal-separation]]\n\n### Definition\nHomomorphic filtering is a technique used to separate components of a signal that are multiplied together in the time domain by transforming them into an additive relationship.\n\n### Application in Echo-Q\nIn arterial pressure waves, the reflected wave (echo) is often a multiplicative component of the complex impedance. Homomorphic separation allows us to isolate the 'source' (heart contraction) from the 'filter' (arterial reflections).\n\n---\n\n## [[entities/grand-unification-of-quantum-algorithms]]\n\n### Thesis\nThis entity represents the convergence of QFT, QSVT, and Block Encoding into a single pipeline for signal processing. \n\n1. **Input**: Signal $s(t)$ encoded via Block Encoding.\n2. **Transform**: QFT applied to reach the frequency domain.\n3. **Processing**: QSVT applies the $\\log$ and deconvolution polynomial.\n4. **Output**: The separated primary wave and echo components.\n\n### Mathematical Goal\nTo realize the operator $\\mathcal{D}$ such that $\\mathcal{D}(s(t)) \\approx p(t)$ with complexity $O(\\text{poly}(\\log N))$.
