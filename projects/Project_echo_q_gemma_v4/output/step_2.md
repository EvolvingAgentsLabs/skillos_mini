# State Constraints and Enforceable Invariants\n\n**Project:** Project Echo-Q (Quantum Cepstral Deconvolution)\n**Phase:** 2 - Constraint Extraction\n**Source Material:** [[concepts/quantum-fourier-transform]], [[concepts/quantum-singular-value-transformation]], [[concepts/block-encoding]], [[concepts/cepstral-analysis]]\n\n## 1. Executive Summary\nThis document defines the formal mathematical and physical invariants required for the Echo-Q algorithm. These constraints serve as the boundary conditions for the Phase 3 implementation. Any deviation from these invariants constitutes a failure of the quantum simulation/algorithm.\n\n---\n\n## 2. Hard Constraints (MUST NOT Violate)\n*These are non-negotiable physical and mathematical laws. Failure to satisfy these results in invalid quantum states or non-physical results.*\n\n### [HC-01] Unitarity of Quantum Operations\n- **Definition:** All quantum transformations applied to the arterial signal state must be represented by unitary operators $U$ where $U^\dagger U = I$.\n- **Requirement:** Any implementation of QFT or QSVT must preserve the norm of the quantum state $\\langle\\psi|\\psi\\rangle = 1$.\n- **Reference:** [[concepts/quantum-fourier-transform]] (Key Property 2: Unitary Evolution).\n\n### [HC-02] No-Cloning Theorem Adherence\n- **Definition:** It is impossible to create an independent, identical copy of an arbitrary unknown quantum state $|\psi\\rangle$.\n- **Requirement:** The deconvolution process must be designed as a transformation of the state, not as a copying mechanism. If state duplication is required for statistical averaging, it must be achieved through state preparation or measurement-based repetition, never via a direct cloning gate.\n- **Reference:** Fundamental Quantum Mechanics Principle.\n\n### [HC-03] Block-Encoding Norm Constraint\n- **Definition:** For a matrix $A$ to be $\\epsilon$-block-encoded in a unitary $U$, the singular values of $A$ must be bounded by the sub-block of $U$.\n- **Requirement:** The scaling factor $\alpha$ used in block encoding must satisfy $\|A\| \\le \\alpha$. The algorithm must not attempt to transform singular values that exceed the normalization factor of the block-encoding unitary.\n- **Reference:** [[concepts/block-encoding]].\n\n### [HC-04] Parseval's Theorem (Energy Conservation)\n- **Definition:** The total energy in the time domain must equal the total energy in the frequency domain.\n- **Requirement:** The QFT must preserve the $\\ell_2$-norm such that $\\sum |x_n|^2 = \\frac{1}{N} \\sum |X_k|^2$ (subject to normalization scaling). Any energy loss during the transform indicates decoherence or gate error.\n- **Reference:** [[concepts/quantum-fourier-transform]].\n\n---\n\n## 3. Soft Constraints (SHOULD Satisfy)\n*These are optimization targets. Violation does not invalidate the physics, but it significantly degrades the clinical utility of the deconvolution.*\n\n### [SC-01] Polynomial Degree Optimization (QSVT Depth)\n- **Requirement:** The degree $d$ of the polynomial $P(x)$ used in QSVT should be minimized to reduce circuit depth.\n- **Target:** $d$ must be large enough to achieve $\\epsilon$-approximation of the deconvolution filter but small enough to stay within the coherence time $T_2$ of the target hardware.\n- **Reference:** [[concepts/quantum-singular-value-transformation]] (Open Question: Optimal degree).\n\n### [SC-02] Spectral Resolution vs. Gate Complexity\n- **Requirement:** The number of qubits $n$ must provide a frequency resolution $\\Delta f$ sufficient to distinguish between the primary pulse and the first reflected wave (echo).\n- **Target:** $\\Delta f \\le |t_{echo} - t_{primary}|^{-1}$. Increasing $n$ improves resolution but increases $O(\\log^2 N)$ gate complexity.\n- **Reference:** [[concepts/quantum-fourier-transform]] (Open Question: High-resolution decomposition).\n\n### [SC-03] Cepstral Stability\n- **Requirement:** The homomorphic separation must ensure that the lifter/liftering process does not introduce spurious high-frequency artifacts into the reconstructed arterial pressure wave.\n- **Target:** The cepstral coefficients should exhibit smooth decay in the quefrency domain.\n- **Reference:** [[concepts/cepstral-analysis]].\n\n---\n\n## 4.
